Rivers in Malaysia

The Malaysian Department of Environment monitors the status of river water quality. The Water Quality Index (WQI) is used to indicate the level of pollution. According to the National Water Quality Standards for Malaysia (NWQS), the WQI measures the amount of dissolved oxygen, biochemical oxygen demand, chemical oxygen demand, ammoniacal nitrogen, suspended solids and pH. The tables below shows the categories and classifications of rivers based on their WQI.
Category WQI
Clean 81 - 100
Slightly Polluted 61 - 80
Polluted 0 - 60
Class WQI

III 51.9 - 76.5

Pollution
Human activities are almost always the cause!

The most polluted river in Malaysia is in Pasir Gudang, Johor. Contributions to pollution include industrial sources, sewage discharge, oxidation ponds, sullage from restaurants and workshops, and direct discharge.

From The Star

In Sungai Petani, Kedah, Sahabat Alam Malaysia (SAM) has found aquatic life to have perished and growth of mangrove tree stunted. They urge the authorities to focus on proper waste disposal system, water treatment and prevent waste oil discharges into the river.

From Consumer Association of Penang

In Kuala Betis near Gua Musang, Kelantan, river pollution has claimed the life of an indigenous people of Temiar, and seven others were hospitalized possibly due to poisoning from drinking water from the river. The water supply was contaminated with toxins.

From Sahabat Alam Malaysia

Just recently, pollution is detected at Sungai Semenyih, and consumers in Klang Valley, including Selangor, Kuala Lumpur and Putrajaya were affected by water supply cuts. The source of pollution is an Industrial Zone and a goose and duck farm.

From The Straits Time

Pollution was detected at the Batu Hampar Dam in Batu Berendam, Melaka, causing Melaka River to turn black and smelly. Dead fish were seen.

From Malay Mail

River pollution was detected in Batang Benar, Nilai, Negeri Sembilan. The pollution is possible caused by duck farms, the Pajam landfill or a chicken egg processing plant.

From Free Malaysia Today

Rivers in Kuantan, Pahang have been polluted with heavy metalsdue to bauxite mining, consequently causing side effects to human health as almost all water consumption in Kuantan is drawn downstream from these mines. An analysis by the Department of Analysis revealed that the rivers are contaminated with mercury, aluminium at several locations in Pahang.

From Clean Malaysia

Pig waste has caused River Kuala Gepai, Perak to turn black for years since no actions taken by any parties. The indigenous people community that has been relying on the river waters for living has been affected for years as well.

From Says

While waiting for the Penang Pig Farming Enactment 2020 to be enforced, a total of 26 pig farms in Kampung Selamat, Tasek Gelugor, Pulau Pinang have been found to have dumped sewage and animal feaces into rivers. The local authority is looking to close down pig farms that do not follow certain rules, including not dumping waste to rivers and not operating in excess capacity in the future.

From Malay Mail

A massive oil spill from a diesel-bitumen premix plant has polluted the Liku River in Sarawak, causing water disruption for up to 12 hours. The Sarawak Conservation ALliance for Natural Environment (SCANE) urged the state goverment to not allow industrial plants situated neat water sources. The current requirement states that all new industrial plants must be at least 500 metres away from rivers, to which SCANE has urged the government to increase the distance threshold.

From Horbill Unleashed

Within a period of 1 month, residents in Selangor has experienced water disruption twice due to river pollution. Water pollution in Sungai Gong, Rawang was caused by a factory, while the pollution in Sungai Semenyih was caused by dumping of waste near the upstream of the river.
